Studies into the Human Genome and Possible Environmental Proponents of Neurodiversity
(These studies are focused on ASD)

Recent studies seek to understand whether autism is built in to the human genome, or if there are genetic predispositions to changes that may be induced by developmental and/or environmental factors. More research is required.

A changing Population of Neurodiverse Humans

Information from the CDC shows, the US has seen a rise in Neurodiversity presented through Autism and ADHD since 2000 and 2003.

  

According to the CDC:

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

  1. About 1 in 44 children has been identified with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) according to estimates from CDC’s Autism and Developmental Disabilities Monitoring (ADDM) Network. [Read article] [8a]
    • ASD is reported to occur in all racial, ethnic, and socioeconomic groups. [Read article]
    • ASD is more than 4 times more common among birth assigned boys than among birth assigned girls. [Read article]
    • About 1 in 6 (17%) children aged 3–17 years were diagnosed with a developmental disability, as reported by parents, during a study period of 2009-2017. These included autism, attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder, blindness, and cerebral palsy, among others. [Read summary]
  2. An estimated 5,437,988 (2.21%) adults in the United States have ASD. [8b]
    • The prevalence of US adults with ASD ranged from a low of 1.97% in Louisiana to a high of 2.42% in Massachusetts.
    • Approximately 4,357,667 (3.62%) male adults were estimated to have ASD, with state estimates ranging from 3.17% of men in South Dakota to 4.01% of men in Massachusetts.
    • Approximately 1,080,322 (0.86%) female adults were estimated to have ASD, with state estimates ranging from 0.72% of women in Arkansas to 0.97% of women in Virginia.

Attention-Deficit / Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) [8c]

  1. Millions of US children have been diagnosed with ADHD
    • The estimated number of children aged 3–17 years ever diagnosed with ADHD, according to a national survey of parents,1 is 6 million (9.8%) using data from 2016-2019.
    • Black, non-Hispanic children and White, non-Hispanic children are more often diagnosed with ADHD (12% and 10%, respectively), than Hispanic children (8%) or Asian, non-Hispanic children (3%)
    • About 3 in 10 children with ADHD had anxiety.
